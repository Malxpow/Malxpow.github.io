Black Box Investigation

I go to a school in West Haven, and it is called ESUMS which is short for Engineering & Science University Magnet School. In my school, there's a class where we have to try and figure out what is in a black box. This black box sometimes has a higher input than output of water and vice versa. I think there is a siphon in the black box. When I saw the Black Box my first thought was to pull water in and see what happens.

Tests

In the first few tests, the black box had higher output than inputs, but other times the input was higher than the output. (Blackbox Tests) In blackbox test the first two test the black box was collecting water then, the second to last test the output was greater then the input. The model that we had to create would have to have a higher output than input, but other times an input higher than the output. My model's key structure is the siphon that can hold the water and have a bigger output than input. Also, the test was the same as Black Box, we would get more or less water than poured in.

Model

Models can be used to simulate what will happen to the real thing, like destroying buildings. A model's limitation is the accuracy of what is being represented. Models are scaled down or in a program for the user to manipulate it so they can see the changes they will do to the real thing. The model of the blackbox that we made was a cardboard box with, a syphon, a funnel, and a small bucket and the bottom, then a tube for the water to exit.
